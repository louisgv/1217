Rule

General

Player initially draw a "hero card" that will represent them in the game. They then proceed to play with the NPC.

The game has 3 rounds, each round has 2 phases: prepare and combat. Winner are determined by the score accumulated in the combat phase. Winner of 2 rounds wins the game.

The game board are split along the x axis, half for player and half for the NPC player. Each half board are then split into 3 rows.

  • Status: Showing the current elemental points accumulated (accept for ETHER), and the avatar.
  • Equipment: Showing what card the player has played.
  • Hand: Showing what card the player is having.

Choosing an avatar

There are 5 unique avatars, each represents an alchemy element (Ether, Water, Fire, Aero, Earth). By choosing an avatar, the player is applying a higher probability of drawing cards of the same element in the prepare phase.

The chosen card will also determine whether if they would be the first to make a move or not in the prepare phase. In short, it is a 5-ways rock-paper-scisor with the NPC's randomly chosen avatar. The player won if their element beats the NPC's element according to the Hierarchy of Element, or if it is a draw.

Prepare phase

In this phase, each side (player and NPC) take turn either:

  • Drawing a card from the central deck
  • Playing a card from their hand

No card will be revealed to the other side accept the hero card. The first to fill up their Equipment slots will initiate the combat phase.

Combat phase

In this phase, each side reveal their card, and the game will calculate who has the higher score by a process called "Nullification".

After the nullification, whoever has the higher combined score win the round and earn a medal. The next round will commence upon which both player will go back to the prepare phase. The elemental points will keep add-on to what they already accumulated on the previous round.

Nullification

Nullification is a sequence of point calculation consisting of 3 steps:

  1. Ether Cancelation: Both side's ETHER point will be subtracted by the amount of Ether from the side with fewer Ether.
  2. Ether Nullification: The remaining ETHER point will be used to subtract from the element with the highest point on the opposite side.
  3. Element Nullification: Based on the Nullification Table, both side's elemental point will be subtracted by the amount of the more powerful element point from the other side.

Hierarchy of Element

  • ETHER beats WATER and FIRE
  • WATER beats FIRE and AERO
  • FIRE beats AERO and EARTH
  • AERO beats EARTH and ETHER
  • EARTH beats ETHER and WATER

Nullification Table

  • ETHER nullifies itself and everything
  • WATER nullifies FIRE
  • FIRE nullifies AERO
  • AERO nullifies EARTH
  • EARTH nullifies WATER
